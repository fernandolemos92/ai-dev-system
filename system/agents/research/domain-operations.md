# RESEARCH MODULE: Domain Operations

This module provides guidance for researching domain operations and technical context.

---

## When To Use This Module

Use this module when the research pass needs to understand:

- how similar problems are technically solved in the domain
- domain-specific operational patterns and constraints
- technical approaches that work well in this context
- technical constraints or considerations that affect the approach
- technical standards or conventions that affect decisions

---

## Domain Operations Research Principles

### Focus on operationally relevant patterns

Domain operations research should surface:

- technical patterns that appear consistently in the domain
- approaches that work well versus approaches that create problems
- domain-specific constraints that affect solution design
- operational considerations that affect maintainability, scalability, or reliability

### Do not confuse technical feature breadth with operational maturity

When researching domain operations:

- look for evidence that approaches actually work in practice
- identify patterns that create operational advantages versus patterns that look impressive but create complexity
- understand what operational excellence looks like in this domain

### Surface technical context honestly

Technical context research should make clear:

- what is well-established versus what is emerging
- what conventions exist versus what is still evolving
- where the domain has settled on patterns versus where significant variation exists
- what operational risks are associated with different approaches

### When technical context affects decisions

If the research is informing technical approach decisions:

- identify what technical approaches are most appropriate for the specific context
- consider modernity, performance, maintainability, compatibility, and execution fit
- avoid choosing technology by habit or convention alone
- look for evidence that approaches work well in contexts similar to the one being planned

---

## Domain-Specific Considerations

### Technical baseline awareness

When researching domain operations:

- understand what technical baselines are common in the domain
- identify what modern approaches are relevant versus what is outdated
- consider whether the technical context calls for frameworked approaches versus simpler solutions

### Operational patterns

Identify patterns in how domain operations are typically structured:

- what architectures are common and why
- what integration patterns work well
- what operational risks are common and how they are typically addressed

---

## Module Selection Logic

Load this module when:

- the project operates in a domain with specific operational patterns
- technical decisions materially depend on domain context
- the decision involves technical approach selection, architecture, or stack choices

Do not load this module when:

- domain operations research is not relevant to the current decision
- the bounded research question is about product or UX decisions rather than technical approach
- the domain context is well-understood and not the bottleneck for the current decision
