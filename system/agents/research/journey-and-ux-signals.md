# RESEARCH MODULE: Journey and UX Signals

This module provides guidance for researching user journeys and surfacing UX signals.

---

## When To Use This Module

Use this module when the research pass needs to understand:

- how users likely behave in a context
- where users may encounter friction or hesitation
- what signals reduce or increase user effort
- user expectations shaped by market education
- how users scan, orient, and make decisions
- trust formation and breakdown points

---

## User Journey Research Principles

### Behavior claims must be labeled honestly

When research surfaces behavioral or UX patterns:

- observed behavior should be called observed only when directly supported
- plausible behavioral interpretation should be called inference
- speculative UX assumptions should be surfaced as unknowns or validation needs

Research must not present behavioral or UX patterns as established fact when the evidence is weak or indirect.

### Focus on end-user experience, not operator experience

When the research subject is user-facing:

- try to understand how the experience likely feels for the end user
- do not stop at what the platform or business claims to offer
- look for signals about how users likely enter a flow, scan, orient, hesitate, or complete tasks
- prioritize behavior, clarity, and trust over raw feature breadth

### Surface friction and trust signals

User journey research should make visible:

- likely user friction points
- trust signals that repeatedly appear in user behavior
- signals that reduce or increase decision effort
- likely sources of confusion or hesitation
- interaction patterns that shape user expectations
- context-of-use observations that may alter behavior

### When evidence is weak

If signals about user behavior are not directly observable:

- label them as inference or validation-needed insight
- do not present them as confirmed user fact
- surface what would need to be validated before building on the assumption

---

## UX Signal Categories

### Entry and orientation

- how do users likely enter the context?
- what do users see first and how do they interpret it?
- where do users need to orient before they can act?

### Decision friction

- where do users likely hesitate or slow down?
- what creates decision fatigue?
- what reduces commitment quality?

### Trust signals

- what signals increase user confidence?
- what signals decrease user confidence?
- what patterns seem to create trust versus anxiety?

### Completion and abandonment

- what patterns are associated with task completion?
- what patterns are associated with abandonment?
- where do users likely drop off versus persist?

---

## Module Selection Logic

Load this module when:

- the project is user-facing with a meaningful user journey
- UX decisions materially affect the product
- user behavior signals affect the current decision
- the research needs to understand user expectations and friction points

Do not load this module when:

- the project is not user-facing
- user journey research is not relevant to the current decision
- the bounded research question is narrowly about technical or business approach
