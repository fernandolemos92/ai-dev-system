# RESEARCH MODULE: Insight Generation

This module provides guidance for synthesizing research findings into actionable insights.

---

## When To Use This Module

Use this module when the research pass needs to:

- convert raw findings into useful insights
- prioritize findings by decision relevance
- translate research into project or discovery implications
- surface what matters most from a body of research

---

## Insight Generation Principles

### Prioritize insight density over information volume

Insight generation should:

- identify the findings that most affect the current decision
- surface insights that change or confirm understanding
- prioritize findings with decision value over findings that are interesting but not actionable

Research that increases volume without increasing clarity or decision quality is not working well.

### Make implications explicit

When generating insights:

- translate findings into implications for the project or decision
- clarify what the research changes about the current understanding
- surface what this supports versus what it does not yet justify

### Surface the decision-relevant takeaways

Insight generation should answer:

- what did the research clarify that was previously uncertain?
- what did the research confirm that can be acted on with confidence?
- what did the research complicate that must be addressed?
- what remains unknown and should not be overclaimed?

---

## Insight Categories

### Confirmed insights

Insights supported by strong evidence and repeated patterns.

These can be acted on with reasonable confidence.

### Qualified insights

Insights supported by plausible inference but requiring some validation.

These should be acted on with appropriate caution and labeled honestly.

### Challenged assumptions

Research findings that contradict or complicate previous assumptions.

These should be surfaced explicitly rather than smoothed over.

### Unknowns and open questions

Research findings that reveal what is still uncertain or needs validation.

These should be surfaced honestly rather than overclaimed.

---

## From Findings to Insights

### Pattern identification

Look for:

- recurring patterns that appear across multiple sources
- important divergences that may matter for the decision
- signals that shape user expectations or choice architecture

### Implication translation

For each significant finding:

- what does this mean for the project or decision?
- what would change in approach if this finding is confirmed?
- what would remain the same?

### Decision framing

Surface insights in a way that helps the next decision:

- what is now clearer than before?
- what can be decided with this grounding?
- what cannot be safely decided yet?

---

## Module Selection Logic

Load this module when:

- the research pass has gathered significant findings that need synthesis
- insight generation is needed to translate research into decision value
- the bounded research question requires turning findings into actionable understanding

Do not load this module when:

- the research is still in evidence gathering phase
- findings are not yet ready for synthesis
- the bounded research question does not require insight generation
