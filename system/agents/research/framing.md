# RESEARCH MODULE: Framing

This module provides guidance for framing research around a real decision.

Research that starts from a vague question produces vague output.

Research that starts from a clear decision question produces useful output.

---

## When To Use This Module

Use this module when the research pass needs to:

- clarify the research question before expanding investigation
- bound scope before expanding research
- identify the smallest decision-relevant framing
- start from the decision need, not from a preferred answer

---

## Framing Principles

### Start from the decision, not the topic

A research question should make clear:

- what decision the research is meant to inform
- what would change if the research produces a clear answer
- what would remain open if the research cannot settle the question

### Prefer narrow and deep over broad and shallow

A bounded research question that produces a clear answer is better than a broad question that produces a long summary.

If the request is broad, identify the smallest decision-relevant framing that would still make the research useful.

### Bound scope explicitly

Define:

- what is included in the research
- what is intentionally excluded
- how deep the investigation should go
- what kinds of sources or evidence are most relevant

Bounded scope should be explicit enough that the reader can understand why certain lines of inquiry were not pursued.

### Keep research proportional to the real decision need

Do not over-research beyond the real need.

If the decision can be made with three strong data points, do not pretend that twenty data points are necessary.

---

## Question Formulation

A well-framed research question should be able to answer:

- What exactly needs to be decided?
- What would the research answer change or confirm?
- What is the smallest scope that would still make the decision safer?
- What would make the next step clearer?

A poorly framed research question:

- asks about a topic instead of a decision
- does not define what would change if an answer is found
- tries to cover everything instead of focusing on what matters
- starts from a preferred conclusion and uses research to validate it

---

## Module Selection Logic

This module (`framing.md`) should be loaded for every research pass.

It is the foundational layer that ensures the rest of the research package operates from a clear decision question.

Do not skip framing for a research pass that seems straightforward.

The decision question should still be explicit even when it feels obvious.
