# RESEARCH MODULE: Benchmark Patterns

This module provides guidance for deriving benchmarks and extracting patterns from reference systems.

---

## When To Use This Module

Use this module when the research pass needs to:

- understand what comparable or competing systems do well
- extract patterns from reference systems
- understand what benchmark quality looks like
- identify what makes certain approaches successful
- distinguish meaningful benchmarks from superficial comparisons

---

## Benchmark Research Principles

### Extract patterns, not features

A benchmark comparison should surface:

- recurring patterns across benchmark systems
- what patterns appear to work consistently
- what patterns appear to be failures or liabilities
- meaningful differentiators between approaches

A benchmark comparison should not be:

- a feature inventory
- a checklist of capabilities
- a list of what exists without judgment of why it matters

### Focus on what the benchmark implies for the user

When evaluating benchmark systems:

- ask what the approach likely means for end users
- ask whether more features are actually better for user outcomes
- look for patterns that improve clarity, confidence, speed, ease, or outcome completion
- do not assume that benchmark breadth automatically translates to user value

### Identify what makes benchmarks successful

Not all benchmark systems are equally relevant or useful.

Research should identify:

- which benchmarks are most relevant to the current decision
- what makes certain benchmarks worth studying
- what patterns are specific to a benchmark versus what patterns are market-wide
- where benchmarks are leading versus lagging user needs

---

## Pattern Extraction

### Recurring patterns

Identify patterns that appear across multiple benchmark systems:

- what do most benchmark systems seem to agree on?
- what patterns appear consistently even across different approaches?
- what patterns seem to be industry conventions?

### Important divergences

Identify meaningful differences between benchmark systems:

- what differentiates the best benchmarks from the rest?
- where do benchmark approaches diverge significantly?
- what divergence matters for the current decision?

### Meaningful differentiators

Distinguish between:

- cosmetic differences that do not affect user outcomes
- meaningful differentiators that change how users experience the system

---

## Module Selection Logic

Load this module when:

- the project needs to understand what good looks like in a space
- comparable systems exist that can inform the approach
- the decision involves quality standards, capability requirements, or approach selection

Do not load this module when:

- no relevant benchmarks exist
- the bounded research question is about something other than approach quality or standard
- benchmarking would create false constraints for a genuinely novel project
