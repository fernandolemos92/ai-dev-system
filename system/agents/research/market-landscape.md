# RESEARCH MODULE: Market Landscape

This module provides guidance for researching market patterns and ecosystem awareness.

---

## When To Use This Module

Use this module when the research pass needs to understand:

- market structure and dynamics
- ecosystem patterns and conventions
- market education of target users
- what the market has taught users to expect
- where the market is heading
- relevant market constraints or opportunities

---

## Market Research Principles

### Focus on decision-relevant market signals

Market research should surface information that materially affects product or project decisions.

A list of every market actor in a space is not useful.

An understanding of which market patterns create user expectations and which create differentiation opportunities is useful.

### Distinguish market reality from market narrative

Market participants often describe their market in terms that favor their position.

Research should look for:

- recurring patterns across multiple market actors
- what users seem to expect based on market education
- where the market creates friction or clarity for users
- meaningful differentiators versus cosmetic differences

### Do not confuse market noise with market signal

When researching market patterns:

- identify recurring patterns, not one-off observations
- look for consensus across multiple references
- surface contradictory signals when they exist
- do not treat a single market actor's claim as market-wide evidence

### Map ecosystem patterns carefully

When the ecosystem has patterns that affect user expectations:

- identify what the ecosystem has standardized
- identify what remains fragmented or inconsistent
- understand why certain patterns became dominant
- look for opportunities where market patterns create space for better approaches

---

## Market Education of Users

A critical question in market research:

What has the market taught users to expect?

This affects:

- what users consider normal
- what creates trust or friction
- what users compare against
- where users have pre-formed assumptions

Research should try to understand market education of users, not just market structure.

---

## Module Selection Logic

Load this module when:

- the project operates in a known market space
- market patterns materially affect product decisions
- user expectations are shaped by market education
- the decision involves market positioning, differentiation, or timing

Do not load this module when:

- the project operates outside existing market categories
- market structure research is not relevant to the current decision
- the bounded research question is narrowly technical
