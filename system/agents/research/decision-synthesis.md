# RESEARCH MODULE: Decision Synthesis

This module provides guidance for producing decision-ready research synthesis.

---

## When To Use This Module

Use this module when the research pass needs to produce:

- a structured research output that supports the next decision
- clear findings that are decision-ready
- honest representation of evidence, inference, and unknowns
- a synthesis that translates research into project or discovery implications

---

## Decision Synthesis Principles

### Produce synthesis, not raw accumulation

Decision synthesis should:

- convert findings into a coherent output that supports the next decision
- make the research decision-ready, not just comprehensive
- surface the most important takeaways with appropriate confidence levels

### Separate evidence from inference

Decision synthesis must distinguish:

- what is directly supported by evidence
- what is a plausible but still interpretive conclusion
- what remains unknown and should not be overstated

This separation is critical when research informs product, UX, or positioning decisions.

### Make unknowns visible

Decision synthesis should explicitly surface:

- what remains uncertain
- what would still require validation
- what should not be concluded from the current research
- what research limitations affect the decision

### Provide decision-relevant framing

Decision synthesis should answer:

- what does this research enable the system to decide with better grounding?
- what does this research prevent the system from deciding safely yet?
- what is the most appropriate next bounded step based on this research?

---

## Output Structure for Decision Synthesis

### Research Goal

What question or decision the research is serving.

This should be explicit and decision-oriented.

### Key Findings

The main findings of the research, prioritized by decision relevance.

Findings should reflect the strongest grounded takeaways, not every observed detail.

### Evidence vs Inference vs Unknowns

Explicit distinction between:

- what is directly supported by evidence
- what is a plausible but still interpretive conclusion
- what remains unknown and should not be overstated

### Risks and Unknowns

What remains uncertain, risky, or unresolved.

This section should explicitly surface what would still require validation rather than being assumed.

### Recommended Next Question

The next best bounded question that should be answered if the goal is to continue reducing ambiguity.

### Recommended Next Step

The most appropriate next action based on the research, remaining advisory.

---

## Quality Standards for Decision Synthesis

### Honest confidence levels

Synthesis should be honest about confidence levels:

- do not upgrade plausible inference to confirmed fact
- do not smooth over unknowns to make the summary sound stronger
- do not treat one example as universal proof

### Decision usefulness

Synthesis should be useful for the next decision:

- what changed based on this research?
- what is now clearer?
- what can be decided safely?

### Reuse across stages

When research findings will be reused across later stages:

- ensure evidence and inference separation remains traceable
- make the synthesis durable for later reference
- surface what is decided versus what remains open clearly

---

## Module Selection Logic

Load this module at the end of every research pass that is meant to inform a decision.

It is the output layer that converts research into a form that supports the next bounded step.

Every research pass that aims to be decision-ready should end with decision synthesis.

Do not load this module when the research is exploratory and not yet meant to produce a decision-ready output.
