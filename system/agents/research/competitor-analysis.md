# RESEARCH MODULE: Competitor Analysis

This module provides guidance for analyzing competitive references and understanding competitive dynamics.

---

## When To Use This Module

Use this module when the research pass needs to:

- understand what competitive systems exist
- analyze how competitors approach similar problems
- identify competitive strengths and weaknesses
- understand competitive positioning and differentiation
- inform product or positioning decisions based on competitive landscape

---

## Competitive Research Principles

### Treat competitor claims as evidence of positioning, not user value

Competitors describe themselves in terms that favor their position.

When analyzing competitors:

- treat what competitors say they do as positioning evidence
- look for what competitors actually do versus what they claim
- identify patterns in how competitors differentiate themselves
- do not automatically treat competitor feature lists as evidence of user value

### Focus on what competitors reveal about user expectations

Competitors have often spent significant time understanding what users need.

Their choices reveal:

- what they believe users expect
- where they believe differentiation is possible
- what market conventions they are following versus breaking
- where they believe user needs are underserved

### Identify meaningful competitive signals

Competitor research should surface:

- patterns in how competitors approach the problem
- what seems to be working for competitors
- what seems to be failing or creating friction
- where competitors are investing versus pulling back
- competitive dynamics that create opportunities or constraints

### Do not confuse competitive awareness with competitive obsession

Understanding the competitive landscape is useful.

Trying to match every competitor move is not useful.

Research should focus on competitive signals that materially affect the current decision, not on comprehensive competitive coverage.

---

## Competitive Positioning

When researching competitive positioning:

- identify where competitors are strong versus where they are weak
- understand what positioning claims competitors make
- assess whether positioning claims match actual capabilities
- look for positioning gaps where differentiation is possible
- understand what differentiation the market seems to reward

---

## Module Selection Logic

Load this module when:

- competitive systems materially affect product or positioning decisions
- the project operates in a space with known competitors
- competitive differentiation is part of the decision
- understanding competitor weaknesses creates opportunities

Do not load this module when:

- no meaningful competitors exist
- the bounded research question is not about competitive dynamics
- competitive research would create anxiety without actionable insight
