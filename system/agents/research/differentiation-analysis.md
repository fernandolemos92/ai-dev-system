# RESEARCH MODULE: Differentiation Analysis

This module provides guidance for analyzing differentiation opportunities and unique value propositions.

---

## When To Use This Module

Use this module when the research pass needs to understand:

- how to differentiate from existing alternatives
- what makes something unique versus commoditized
- where differentiation opportunities exist
- how to communicate unique value honestly
- what differentiation the market rewards versus ignores

---

## Differentiation Research Principles

### Focus on meaningful differentiation

Differentiation research should identify:

- differences that actually matter to users or buyers
- differentiation that creates real value versus differentiation that sounds good but does not matter
- where differentiation is sustainable versus where it is easily copied

### Do not confuse differentiation with marketing claims

When analyzing differentiation:

- separate what competitors claim as differentiation from what actually differentiates them
- identify differentiation that is structural versus differentiation that is cosmetic
- look for differentiation that is difficult to replicate quickly

### Understand what differentiation the market rewards

Differentiation research should surface:

- what differentiators seem to be valued in the market
- what differentiators create genuine preference versus what differentiators are ignored
- where differentiation creates pricing power versus where it does not

### Be honest about differentiation limits

Not every project can have meaningful differentiation in every dimension.

Research should be honest about:

- where meaningful differentiation is possible
- where differentiation is limited or constrained
- what differentiation exists versus what is aspirational

---

## Sources of Differentiation

### Product differentiation

- features or capabilities that are unique or significantly better
- user experience that is meaningfully better
- performance, reliability, or quality that is materially better

### Positioning differentiation

- target audience that is underserved by alternatives
- value proposition that is genuinely different
- market positioning that creates clear preference for a specific audience

### Operational differentiation

- execution quality that is materially better
- speed, reliability, or cost structure that creates advantages
- operational excellence that competitors cannot easily match

### Experience differentiation

- user experience that creates genuine preference
- trust, confidence, or ease that competitors do not provide
- emotional or functional connection that competitors do not create

---

## Module Selection Logic

Load this module when:

- differentiation decisions materially affect the product or positioning
- the project operates in a space with alternatives that must be distinguished
- understanding what makes something unique is relevant to the decision

Do not load this module when:

- the project is in a space where differentiation research is not relevant
- the bounded research question is about technical approach or execution quality rather than positioning
- differentiation is not the current decision bottleneck
