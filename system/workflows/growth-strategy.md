# WORKFLOW: growth-strategy

This workflow defines how the system should perform governed growth-oriented strategic work when positioning, messaging, SEO, CRO, analytics logic, or funnel structure materially matters.

It is a macro workflow contract.

It does not replace governance, live state, or bounded skill execution.

---

## Purpose

Use this workflow to shape how the product is communicated, discovered, measured, or optimized when that work genuinely matters to the current scope.

This workflow exists to improve:

- positioning clarity
- messaging coherence
- SEO direction
- conversion logic
- analytics and funnel thinking
- downstream go-to-market-facing implementation clarity

---

## Workflow Role

This workflow governs growth-oriented strategy as a real traversal step, not as decorative business support.

When it is materially required, it must be explicitly entered and materially completed rather than merely mentioned.

That means:

- the need for strategy work must be explicit
- the relevant specialists must be explicitly evaluated and used when materially needed
- any durable strategic direction must be synthesized clearly
- a `Strategy` artifact must be created when durable direction is required
- downstream progression must not pretend the strategy question was resolved when it was only acknowledged

Recognition of messaging or funnel importance is not the same as completion of this workflow.

---

## When This Workflow Is Materially Needed

Use this workflow when one or more of the following is true:

- the project includes a landing page, acquisition surface, or discoverability concern
- value communication materially affects success
- messaging ambiguity could weaken downstream delivery or review
- SEO, CRO, or measurement logic is part of the real scope
- analytics or funnel assumptions materially shape what should be built
- the user explicitly wants strategic communication, growth direction, conversion logic, or discoverability direction
- research or product-definition output concluded that growth-strategy is materially required before later progression

Do not use this workflow decoratively.

Do not make it mandatory for every project.

If communication, discoverability, conversion, and measurement concerns are not materially part of the current scope, this workflow may remain inactive — but that conclusion must be explicit.

For user-facing products with acquisition, trust, or messaging-sensitive surfaces, this workflow must always be explicitly evaluated.

When it is materially relevant, silent bypass is not valid.

---

## Relationship To Product-Definition Workflow

This workflow often operates as a materially relevant domain traversal inside the broader `product-definition` path.

Accordingly:

- `discover-missing-context` may identify that growth-strategy is materially required
- `structured-research` may strengthen or confirm that need
- `product-definition` must not silently bypass this workflow when it is materially blocking safe PRD or later downstream quality
- `plan-tasks` and `prepare-handoff` may consume the resulting direction later

This workflow does not replace product-definition.

It provides governed strategic maturity inside it.

If the current execution line already determined that growth-strategy is materially required, downstream PRD or execution-preparation work must not proceed as though the requirement had already been satisfied.

---

## Typical Governed Span

This workflow usually operates after product definition is reasonably stable and before implementation or go-live shaping.

It often relates to:

- `validation`
- `strategy`
- `tasks`
- `handoff`

In some cases it may become materially relevant before PRD safety is fully established, especially when value communication, acquisition framing, discoverability, or funnel assumptions still materially shape safe scope framing.

This workflow may shape how later work is framed, but it does not replace product definition, task planning, or review.

---

## Canonical Skills and Surrounding Pieces

This workflow requires an explicit bounded traversal step for growth-strategy itself.

The canonical traversal skill for first-pass governed growth-strategy synthesis is:

- `define-growth-strategy`

This bounded synthesis step must evaluate the materially relevant growth specialists and produce direction-level strategic output for the active execution line.

This workflow does not replace bounded skill execution.

It may influence or be influenced by:

- product-definition work
- validation work
- task decomposition
- handoff framing
- review reasoning when strategy is in scope

These bounded governed pieces do not replace this workflow.

They operate inside or around it.

---

## Growth-Strategy Traversal Entry Rule

When this workflow becomes materially required, the system must enter it through the canonical bounded synthesis step:

- `define-growth-strategy`

A correct traversal entry must:

- identify which growth specialists are materially relevant
- resolve those specialists through the registry/runtime bridge
- synthesize their contribution into bounded strategic direction
- determine whether a durable `Strategy` artifact is required
- stop after the bounded growth-strategy moment

This workflow must not treat scattered growth commentary, specialist naming, or later execution planning as a substitute for first-pass strategic synthesis.

Direction must happen first.

Execution may happen later only through the correct governed path.

---

## Mandatory Specialist Evaluation

Relevant specialists may include:

- `positioning`
- `copy-messaging`
- `seo`
- `cro`
- `analytics`

These specialists remain bounded support inside the workflow.

They do not become a parallel commercial governance layer.

However, when this workflow is materially required, the relevant strategic specialists must not be silently bypassed.

A correct traversal should be able to state, honestly:

- which strategic specialists were materially relevant
- which were actually used
- which strategic question was clarified
- what downstream consequence now follows

If a materially relevant specialist is unavailable for safe traversal, this workflow must stop honestly with that limitation made explicit.

It must not pretend completion by approximation alone.

---

## Required Synthesis Behavior

This workflow must produce explicit strategic synthesis.

That synthesis should make clear, at minimum:

- what strategic question was being clarified
- what direction is now explicit
- what remains assumption versus validated fact
- what communication, discoverability, funnel, or measurement implications now exist
- what must remain consistent downstream
- whether a durable `Strategy` artifact was required and created

Specialist fragments alone are not enough.

Loose “growth ideas” alone are not enough.

Strategy work is not mature until the resulting direction is synthesized clearly enough to affect downstream work intentionally.

---

## Expected Output

When this workflow is used, the system should aim to produce some or all of the following:

- a `Strategy` artifact when durable strategic direction is warranted
- clearer value communication
- more explicit messaging assumptions
- bounded SEO or discoverability guidance
- more explicit measurement or funnel assumptions
- downstream implications for tasking, handoff, PRD, or implementation when applicable

If this workflow is materially required and produces no downstream consequence, it should say so explicitly rather than pretending traversal value by ceremony.

---

## When A Strategy Artifact Is Warranted

A durable `Strategy` artifact is usually warranted when one or more of the following is true:

- positioning or messaging decisions will affect multiple later steps
- SEO, CRO, or analytics logic needs durable traceability
- implementation or review will depend on stable strategic assumptions
- the strategic layer is substantial enough that conversational guidance would be fragile
- the project includes a meaningful acquisition, conversion, or measurement surface
- research or product-definition output already established that communication or funnel quality is a key differentiator

A `Strategy` artifact is not required for every project.

If the strategic need is narrow, local, and adequately captured in the current bounded step, the system may proceed without materializing one.

But when durable downstream dependence exists, the artifact should not be skipped.

---

## Canonical Strategy Artifact Placement

When this workflow produces a durable `Strategy` artifact, that artifact must be materialized in the canonical active strategy location:

`project/strategy/active/`

It must not be created in:

- `project/`
- the root of `project/strategy/`
- another artifact family
- `done/` or `archived/` as an initial materialization path

If durable strategic direction was materially required but the artifact was not created canonically, the workflow must not be treated as operationally complete.

---

## Strategic Truthfulness Rule

This workflow must clearly separate:

- directly grounded evidence
- plausible strategic inference
- still-open uncertainty

It must not disguise speculative positioning, messaging, or funnel logic as validated truth.

A plausible strategy direction may still be useful.

But it must remain honestly framed as direction, not proof.

This matters especially for:

- positioning statements
- CTA logic
- conversion assumptions
- SEO priorities
- analytics assumptions

This workflow must also avoid pseudo-precision.

It must not invent or silently harden:

- target conversion rates
- funnel percentages
- SEO ranking expectations
- acquisition assumptions
- analytics thresholds
- performance targets for strategic claims
- exact messaging performance claims

unless those points are already materially grounded by evidence or upstream governed decisions.

Useful strategic direction is allowed.

Fabricated strategic certainty is not.

---

## Downstream Impact

When used correctly, this workflow should materially improve downstream work.

Typical impacts include:

- clarifying what the product is trying to communicate
- making landing-page or acquisition-facing work less generic
- surfacing analytics or funnel logic early enough to affect delivery
- helping tasks and handoffs reflect real strategic priorities
- reducing the chance that measurement needs are discovered too late
- helping review assess not only functionality, but strategic coherence when strategy is in scope

If the workflow produces no meaningful downstream consequence, it should say so explicitly rather than pretending that strategic ceremony added value.

---

## Readiness Signal

This workflow is sufficiently closed for the current pass when the strategic direction is explicit enough to guide downstream work without pretending to resolve every commercial uncertainty.

At minimum, the system should be able to state:

- what strategic question was being clarified
- what direction is now explicit
- what remains assumption versus validated fact
- whether a durable `Strategy` artifact exists in `project/strategy/active/` or is unnecessary
- what downstream implementation, task, handoff, PRD, or review consequences now follow
- whether the workflow materially completed or remains blocked

This workflow does not require market certainty.

It requires enough bounded strategic clarity to keep downstream work from improvising critical communication or measurement decisions blindly.

---

## Completion and Stop Rule

Growth-strategy completion is a bounded workflow moment.

When this workflow materially completes, the system may:

- finalize strategic synthesis
- create the durable `Strategy` artifact when required
- update `PROJECT_STATE.md` coherently to reflect the strategy-complete moment

It must then stop and return control.

This workflow must not:

- auto-open `experience-direction`
- auto-open `plan-tasks`
- auto-open `prepare-handoff`
- auto-open implementation work
- treat improved strategic direction as permission to continue in the same turn

The correct result of this workflow is:

- growth-strategy materially completed
- artifact materially present when required
- one coherent post-strategy state
- explicit return of control

Nothing later.

---

## Must Not

This workflow must not:

- become mandatory for every project
- pretend speculative positioning is validated fact
- replace core product definition or governed review
- create decorative strategy artifacts with no downstream role
- disguise unresolved commercial uncertainty as settled truth
- widen the scope beyond the bounded project need
- be treated as “implicitly satisfied” merely because someone mentioned messaging, SEO, or analytics
- auto-continue into later workflow stages after strategy closure
